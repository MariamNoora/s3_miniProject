    What is a Landslide?

    A landslide is the movement of rock, earth, or debris down a slope. It‚Äôs a type of mass wasting, which denotes any downward movement of soil and rock under the direct influence of gravity. Landslides can be sudden and devastating, often triggered by intense or prolonged rainfall, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, deforestation, or human activities like construction and mining.

    In Kerala, landslides are a recurring hazard, especially in the monsoon months. The hilly districts of Idukki, Wayanad, and Pathanamthitta are highly vulnerable. Major incidents include:

    • 2020 Pettimudi landslide ‚Äì Claimed 66 lives after heavy rainfall in Munnar's tea estate area.
    • 2018 Kavalappara landslide ‚Äì Triggered by intense rain, burying multiple homes and killing dozens in Malappuram.
    • 2019 Wayanad landslides ‚Äì Severe slope failures caused road blocks and massive displacement of families.

    These incidents underline the importance of early warning systems, responsible land use, and public awareness to reduce risk and save lives.

    Common Causes of Landslides

    • Heavy or prolonged rainfall
    • Deforestation and land-use changes
    • Earthquakes and tremors
    • Construction and mining activity
    • Soil saturation and slope instability

    Quick Safety Tips

    Stay alert during heavy rains or warnings

    Evacuate steep slopes immediately during risk periods

    Report unusual cracks or ground movement

    Keep emergency supplies and contact numbers ready

    Monitor government authorities for warnings and follow their instructions.

    Stay clear of trees, powerline, and poles
